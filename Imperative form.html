We use the impertive form to express a command or request. The subject you (singular or plural) is understood but not expressed.


Come back later
Wait outside

We form the negative impertive with don´t.


Don´t come back later.
Don´t wait outside

We use please at the beginning or end of an IMPERATIVE sentence to make it more polite.


Please come back later.
Wait outside, please.
